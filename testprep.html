My Three-Part Approach

I help my students see improvements by:

1

Frequent Assessment

Quizzing them early and often on content they need to know. We track progress together so you can see growth over time.

2

Strategy Development

Developing a personalized test-taking strategy that plays to their strengths.

3

Practice Simulation

Ensuring they test the way they practice through regular "mock" exams. No surprises on test day.

Tests I Prepare Students For

I've spent the most time with these tests, but each is unique and many skills overlap

ACT
College Admission

The ACT moves fast - it tests what you know in English, Math, Reading, and Science, all under tight time limits. Doing well means working smart and staying sharp.

  • Smart time management strategies
  • How to tackle science and data questions
  • Full-length practice to build skills and confidence
SAT
College Admission

The SAT is all about how you read, think, and problem-solve. It focuses on reading, writing, and math. Success comes from a mix of knowledge and approach.

  • Reading with purpose and spotting evidence
  • Math strategies that make tough problems easier
  • Using process of elimination to make confident choices
ISEE
Independent School

The Independent School Entrance Exam is used for admission to many private and independent schools. Tests verbal reasoning, reading, and math.

  • Age-appropriate strategies
  • Verbal reasoning skills
  • Essay writing practice
SSAT
Independent School

The Secondary School Admission Test is another pathway to private school admission. Includes quantitative, verbal, and reading sections.

  • Synonym & analogy practice
  • Quantitative comparisons
  • Writing sample preparation

Have a different test in mind? Feel free to inquire - many skills transfer across standardized tests.
